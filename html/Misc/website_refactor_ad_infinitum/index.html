I wonder how much time I will waste cumulatively remaking my website. I've decided to start formally marking down my thoughts when I change it to double down on the wasting time.

I've established personal websites are dumb, but here I am again; another year, another website refactor.

My enthusiasm for emacs led me to look into Emacs Lisp which caused a deep dive into the very interesting world of lisp , and so I've decided to switch to Common Lisp for my simple SSG. (There might be some good reasons for this: more expressive, ANSI standard, lindy, etc. but really I was mostly just curious & wanted to challenge myself to learn something new. I also really came around to the Lambda Calculus style/ s-expressions. Surely not worth the time investment, but what ultimately is)

The more anachronistic and inscrutable the better I guess. (It is nice to be able to permanently live in emacs.)
